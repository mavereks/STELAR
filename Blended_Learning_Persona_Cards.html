Blended Learning Training Program

User Persona Cards for Needs Analysis

Persona 1
The COVID Pivot Instructor
👩‍🏫 Dana
Associate Professor of History 12 years at St. Thomas
Background

Dana has been teaching face-to-face courses for over a decade and was highly effective in the classroom. When COVID hit, she scrambled to move her courses online with minimal preparation. She made it work, but it was stressful and felt like a compromise. Since returning to campus, she's avoided online teaching entirely.

Goals
  • Regain confidence in online teaching as a legitimate, quality experience
  • Learn intentional course design rather than reactive adaptation
  • Understand what's changed in tools and best practices since 2020
Pain Points
  • Associates online teaching with chaos and burnout
  • Feels behind colleagues who embraced online learning
  • Worried students will have a lesser experience online
  • Doesn't know where to start or what she doesn't know
"I survived COVID teaching, but I wouldn't call it good teaching. If I have to do it again, I want to do it right."
What Success Looks Like

Dana completes the training feeling that online teaching can be as engaging and rigorous as her in-person courses. She has a clear framework and practical tools to design with intention.

Experience: Moderate (unplanned) Time: Moderate Motivation: Rebuild confidence
Persona 2
The Refresher Seeker
👨‍💼 Marcus
Professor of Business Analytics 8 years at St. Thomas
Background

Marcus went through STELAR's course design process four years ago and taught online successfully for two years. He's been focused on research lately and hasn't updated his online courses. He knows things have evolved and wants to modernize his approach without starting from scratch.

Goals
  • Learn what's new in tools, policies, and pedagogy
  • Update existing courses with fresh engagement strategies
  • Improve efficiency in course maintenance and delivery
Pain Points
  • Doesn't want to sit through basics he already knows
  • Unsure which of his current practices are now outdated
  • Limited time—needs targeted, efficient learning
  • Wants to know "what's changed" without hunting for it
"I know the fundamentals. Just tell me what's new and what I should stop doing."
What Success Looks Like

Marcus quickly identifies 3-5 meaningful updates to implement in his existing courses. He feels current and confident without having invested excessive time.

Experience: High Time: Low Motivation: Stay current
Persona 3
The Course Converter
👩‍⚕️ Patricia
Clinical Associate Professor, School of Nursing 6 years at St. Thomas
Background

Patricia is an excellent in-person instructor known for dynamic discussions and hands-on learning. Her department is moving her pharmacology course to a blended format to accommodate working nurses in the graduate program. She's committed but anxious about losing what makes her teaching effective.

Goals
  • Translate her engaging in-person pedagogy to online/blended formats
  • Maintain clinical rigor and student interaction
  • Learn specific strategies for health sciences content online
Pain Points
  • Fears online will feel "flat" compared to her classroom energy
  • Unsure how to handle sensitive clinical discussions asynchronously
  • Concerned about academic integrity with online assessments
  • Needs discipline-specific examples, not generic advice
"My students say my class is where concepts finally click. How do I make that happen through a screen?"
What Success Looks Like

Patricia designs a blended course that maintains high engagement and clinical relevance. She discovers new techniques that actually enhance certain learning objectives.

Experience: High (in-person) Time: Moderate Motivation: Translate skills
Persona 4
The True Novice
🧑‍🎓 Jordan
Newly hired Lecturer, English Department Starting fall semester
Background

Jordan just completed a PhD and landed their first full-time teaching position. They've been a TA and taught a few face-to-face sections, but never designed a course from scratch and never used Canvas. They're eager to do well but overwhelmed by everything they need to learn simultaneously.

Goals
  • Understand the basics of online/blended course design
  • Learn Canvas and other institutional tools from the ground up
  • Know what's expected and what "good" looks like at St. Thomas
  • Build confidence before the first day of class
Pain Points
  • Doesn't know what they don't know
  • Overwhelmed by new job, new city, new systems all at once
  • Afraid of making mistakes that affect students or their reputation
  • Needs clear guidance, not open-ended exploration
"Just tell me what I need to do and I'll do it. I don't want to mess this up."
What Success Looks Like

Jordan starts the semester with a functional, well-organized course and clear understanding of expectations. They know where to get help when questions arise.

Experience: Low Time: High Motivation: Learn foundations
Persona 5
The Experienced Outsider
👨‍💻 Kenji
Associate Professor of Computer Science Starting spring semester
Background

Kenji comes from a large state university where he taught online for six years and helped develop their computer science online degree program. He's confident in his pedagogy and has strong opinions about what works. He chose St. Thomas for its mission and smaller community but needs to understand local systems and culture.

Goals
  • Quickly learn St. Thomas-specific tools, standards, and processes
  • Understand institutional expectations without redundant basic training
  • Find where his expertise can contribute and where he needs to adapt
  • Connect with colleagues and support resources
Pain Points
  • Frustrated by training that assumes he's a beginner
  • Needs to know "how we do it here" vs. general best practices
  • May have workflows that don't align with institutional standards
  • Wants efficiency—doesn't have patience for slow onboarding
"I've designed dozens of online courses. What I need to know is how St. Thomas wants things done."
What Success Looks Like

Kenji efficiently onboards to institutional systems, understands where his expertise transfers and where adaptation is needed, and feels like a valued contributor rather than a student.

Experience: High Time: Low Motivation: Institutional fit
Persona 6
The Assigned Adjunct
👩‍⚖️ Maria
Adjunct Instructor, School of Social Work First semester
Background

Maria is a practicing clinical social worker who was recruited to teach one online section of an introductory course. She has professional expertise but limited teaching experience and no experience with online instruction. She's excited but has very limited time outside her full-time job to prepare.

Goals
  • Get up to speed quickly with minimal time investment
  • Understand the essentials of teaching the pre-designed course
  • Know the bare minimum expectations and where to get help
  • Avoid embarrassing mistakes with students or technology
Pain Points
  • Severely limited time—works full-time in her practice
  • May be teaching a course designed by someone else
  • Feels disconnected from the institution and colleagues
  • Doesn't know what resources exist or how to access them
"I have about 10 hours before the semester starts. What absolutely must I know?"
What Success Looks Like

Maria feels prepared enough to teach confidently on day one. She knows the critical must-dos, has a quick-reference guide, and knows exactly who to contact when issues arise.

Experience: Low Time: Very Low Motivation: Survive & succeed
Persona 7
The Program Administrator
👨‍💼 Robert
Director of MBA Program / Associate Professor 15 years at St. Thomas
Background

Robert is a seasoned faculty member who took on program director responsibilities three years ago. The MBA program is expanding its blended offerings, and he's now responsible for ensuring quality and consistency across 12 faculty members with wildly different skill levels and attitudes toward online teaching.

Goals
  • Develop personal competency to model and evaluate good practice
  • Learn how to train, support, and coach faculty at all levels
  • Establish quality benchmarks for blended courses program-wide
  • Know institutional resources to refer faculty appropriately
  • Handle resistant or struggling faculty constructively
Pain Points
  • Caught between administrative demands and faculty relationships
  • Some faculty resist feedback or see online as "lesser"
  • Lacks formal training in instructional coaching
  • Needs to support everyone from novices to experts
  • Limited budget and time for faculty development
"I can't just tell faculty to 'make it better.' I need to show them how—or connect them with someone who can."
What Success Looks Like

Robert has frameworks and resources to support faculty across the experience spectrum. He can identify struggling courses early, provide constructive guidance, and knows when to escalate to STELAR or other support.

Experience: Moderate Time: Low Motivation: Enable others
